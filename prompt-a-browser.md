# Path A — The Browser Prompt

Copy everything inside the box and paste it as your **first message** to Claude at **claude.ai**. Answer its questions one at a time. At the end it hands you two files to save. No installing anything.

---

```
You are helping me build a "business memory" for you: a small set of plain files
that hold what you need to know about my business, so that I never have to
re-explain it to you again. I am a business owner, not a developer. Keep
everything in plain English, and never assume I know technical terms.

Work in this order, and do not skip ahead.

1. Interview me first. Ask me ONE question at a time and wait for my answer
   before asking the next one. Cover, roughly in this order:
     - What my business is, and what I actually do day to day.
     - Who my customers are, and who my key suppliers or partners are.
     - The handful of names, terms, or facts you would otherwise get wrong.
     - A few important decisions I have already made, and why (so you don't
       reopen settled questions later).
     - How I want you to work with me, and how I want you to write (plain,
       blunt, formal, warm, however I say).
   Ask a follow-up when my answer is thin. Stop interviewing when you have
   enough to be genuinely useful, not when you have hit some quota.

2. Then write me TWO files, in markdown, and show me each one before we're done:
     - CLAUDE.md (my constitution): the short rules file you should read first
       in every session. Who I am, how to work with me, how to write, and the
       standing habit from point 4.
     - Business.md (my living memory): what my business is, my customers, my
       suppliers, my key terms, and a short "Decisions" list.

3. Keep secrets OUT. No passwords, no account keys, no card or bank numbers.
   Prices and ordinary business facts are fine; anything that would unlock an
   account is not. If I start to hand you one, stop me.

4. Put this habit into the constitution, in your own words: at the end of every
   working session, ask me whether anything from today should be saved into
   these files. Propose the exact lines. I approve or change them. Nothing is
   saved without my okay.

5. When you hand me the files, tell me plainly how to use them: save both
   somewhere I will find them, and at the start of any future session, paste
   them in or attach them before we work. Then tell me the truth about the
   limits: this is a strong starter, but you do not yet know which of my facts
   matter most, and these files will drift out of date unless I keep the habit
   in point 4. Deciding what is load-bearing, and keeping it current, is the
   real work.

Begin by telling me in one sentence what we are about to do together, then ask
me your first question.
```

---

**That's it.** Save the two files it gives you. From now on, paste them in at the start of any session and watch it walk in already knowing your business.
